### Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio
- The P/E ratio is a valuation metric that compares a company's stock price to its earnings per share (EPS).
- High P/E Ratio: May indicate overvaluation.
- Low P/E Ratio: May suggest undervaluation.

### Stock Market
- A marketplace where stocks, bonds, and other securities are bought and sold.
- Major stock exchanges include:
  1. New York Stock Exchange (NYSE)
  2. NASDAQ
  3. London Stock Exchange (LSE)

### Market Trends
- Bull Market: A period of rising stock prices, often lasting months or years.
- Bear Market: A decline of 20% or more in stock prices, usually due to economic downturns.

### Diversification
- An investment strategy that spreads investments across different asset classes, industries, or geographies to reduce risk.

### Dividends
- Regular payments made by a corporation to its shareholders, usually from profits.

### Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF)
- An investment fund traded on stock exchanges, holding a diversified portfolio of assets.
- ETFs provide liquidity and diversification benefits.
